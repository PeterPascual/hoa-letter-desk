Texas HOA Violation Letters: Notice, Cure & Fine Requirements

Tex. Prop. Code §209.006–.007 · For boards of self-managed associations

Texas is one of the stricter states on HOA enforcement procedure. Before a property owners' association can fine an owner or suspend privileges, Chapter 209 of the Property Code requires specific written notice — and skipping a step can make the fine unenforceable.

What Texas law requires before a fine

  • Written notice must be sent by certified mail before a fine or suspension (§209.006).
  • The notice must describe the violation and, if it is curable, tell the owner they may cure it within a reasonable period to avoid the penalty.
  • The notice must inform the owner of the right to request a hearing before the board on or before the 30th day after the notice (§209.007).
  • Fines must be reasonable in light of the violation and consistent with the association's governing documents.

The escalation ladder courts expect

Whatever the statute says, judges, mediators, and ombudsmen look for the same paper trail: a courtesy notice, then a formal violation notice with a cure period, then a final notice or hearing, and only then a fine — each dated, each retained with its delivery proof. Boards lose enforcement disputes on skipped steps far more often than on the merits of the violation.

Texas HOA enforcement FAQ

Does a Texas HOA violation notice have to be sent by certified mail?

Yes — §209.006 requires the pre-fine notice to be sent by certified mail. Many boards also send a copy by regular mail or email, but certified mail with return receipt is what proves the notice clock started.

How long does a Texas homeowner have to cure a violation?

The statute requires a reasonable cure period for curable violations and the notice must say so. Thirty days is the common practice, and an association's own documents may set a specific period.

Can a Texas HOA fine without offering a hearing?

No. The owner must be told of the right to request a hearing within 30 days of the notice, and enforcement generally waits until that process runs its course.
